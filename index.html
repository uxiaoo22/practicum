PROJECT LOGIC

Why evaluate the subway option?

Project Focus

We evaluate a proposed subway option along Roosevelt Boulevard. By 2040, the boulevard will be redesigned with one major transit mode — BRT, light rail, or subway — but the decision is still undetermined.

Our goal is to assess the overall impacts of the subway option and provide clear, objective evidence for decision-making.

Key findings

01

Inner stations carry the heaviest burden

Broad-Erie (0.975) and North 9th (0.953) are the most socially vulnerable. Nearly half of households are car-free — entirely transit-dependent.

02

Bustleton is the corridor's crash hotspot

18 serious/fatal crashes in 2024 — nearly 3× the corridor mean of 6.2. Subway-induced car reduction delivers immediate safety co-benefits.

03

Outer stations face a healthcare access crisis

Southampton (2.42 km) and Red Lion (1.80 km) are farthest from any healthcare facility. Subway dramatically cuts effective travel time to medical resources.
